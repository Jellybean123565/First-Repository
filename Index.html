Ice Skating

Learn to skate!

How to Skate:

Basics

  1. Make sure your skates are upright when you stand in them.
  2. When you get on the ice, (if you just started skating) hold the railing and start walking forward on the ice.
  3. You will soon get the sensation that walking on ice is a pretty hard.
  4. Turn one of your skates (your dominant foot) out, and make an L shape with your feet.
  5. Push back with the foot that is not pointing forward. You should be moving forward, gliding.
  6. You should keep going with this if you don’t want to fall.
  7. You should switch feet everytime. This process will soon get easier, and you will be able to skate!

Good Luck!

Intermediate

  1. If you know how to skate, and know a few simple things about skating, you are qualified for this level. Intermediate’s are people who are in the ”Basic” levels.
  2. In these levels, you learn simple things like:
    • Skating on one leg, forwards and backwards
    • Outside and inside three turns
    • Shoot the duck
    • Two foot spin
    • Spiral
    • Lunge
    • Forward to Backward Trasition
    • Other
  3. If You have masted these, then Congratulations! You are qualified for advanced.

Congrats! You’ve worked hard. Give yourself a break. Then try the next level. Advanced!

Advanced

  1. After you have finished the ”Basics” or “Alpha, Beta, Gamma”, then you are ready for Advanced.
  2. When you‘re in the Advanced levels, you are in the “FreeSkate” or “Freestyle” levels.
  3. In these levels, you learn things like:
    • One foot spin
    • Edges
    • Half flip
    • Ballet Jump
    • Back Spin
    • Salchow
    • Toe loop
    • Change foot spin
    • Sit spin
    • Camel spin
    • Other
  4. If you have done these, Good Work! You can move on to Competative teams.
